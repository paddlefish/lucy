Violet Terms and Definitions

Flower Shapes:

single: one layer of petals, i.e., five petals in all
semi-double: flower which has more petals than a single, but not enough to be called a double.
double: flower with at least two layers of petals

pansy: flower on which the petals are unequal size, two small on the top and 3 large on the bottom
star: flower on which the petals are of equal size
frilled: Also called ruffled, undulate or wavy. describes a flower which has a wavy appearance.

Flower Color Patterns or Markings:

bicolor: describes a flower which exhibits two colors.
edged: describes a flower with an edge that differs in color from the rest of the flower.
geneva: describes a flower which has a white edge.
pinwheel: also called striped. Describes a flower which has bi-color rays which emanate from the center of the flower and continue to the edge of the petals. The pinwheel bloom pattern is most often associated with chimeras.
two-tone: describes a flower which exhibits two different tones of the same color.

Leaf Shapes:

ovate: leaf which is ovate or oval-shaped
plain: sometimes called standard or boy-type, describes a leaf which has no remarkable characteristics
quilted: describes a leaf on which the areas between the veins are raised, giving the leaf a puffy, distinctively quilted look. An otherwise plain leaf, with only slightly raised areas between the veins, is called a tailored leaf.
tailored: describes a plain leaf on which the areas between the veins are slightly raised.
serrated: also called dentate or fringed. Describes a leaf with a serrated or saw-toothed edge.
red reverse: describes a leaf which has a reddish-purple underside.
variegated: a leaf which exhibits two distinct colors (i.e., green and white) or two distinct tones of the same color (i.e., green and light green).

Sizes:

standard: describes the most common plant size or growth habit for African Violets. Standard African Violets are single-crowned and have a diameter of 8 inches or more.
semi-miniature: also known as compact. Any African Violet which measures 6 to 8 inches in diameter.
miniature: a miniature is typically 4 to 6 inches in diameter and is normally grown in a 2-inch pot.
